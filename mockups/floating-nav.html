Floating section nav — variations

Right-edge vertical scrollspy variants for the landing page's three core sections: Experience, Projects Timeline, and Projects. Each preview scrolls independently inside its frame so you can feel how the indicator tracks the active section — scroll inside any frame, or click an indicator to jump.

A — Minimal dots, label on hover

smallest footprint

Tiny dots, active fills green with a soft halo. Section names appear only on hover. Pros: calmest visually, blends with minimal design. Cons: users can't tell what each dot represents until they hover — discoverability cost.

B — Dots + persistent text labels

most discoverable

Section names sit beside dots all the time. Pros: instantly readable, no hover required. Cons: widest variant — eats horizontal space. May feel heavy on a minimal page.

C — Vertical rail with traveling fill

progress + nav combined

A thin rail with tick-dots for each section. The green fill grows downward as you scroll, so it doubles as a scroll-progress indicator. Labels sit to the left of the rail. Pros: reads as a true timeline, matches the existing career/project timelines on the page. Cons: slightly more visual weight.

D — Numbered pills

tactile, button-like

Each section is a numbered circle. Active pill turns green with a drop shadow. Names appear on hover. Pros: obviously clickable, easy targets. Cons: the numbers don't carry meaning (1, 2, 3 are arbitrary) — might feel like a stepper/wizard, which this isn't.

E — Floating pill island (bottom-right)

single chip · novel

One small chip in the corner showing "2 / 3 · Projects Timeline" plus up/down chevrons. Pros: very small footprint, doesn't touch the side rail of content, the chevrons make jump-to-next-section deliberate. Cons: harder to glance at the whole structure of the page — you only ever see one section name at a time.

F — Compact rail with hover tooltips

middle ground

Like C but tighter — the rail is fixed-height (regardless of section count), section nodes sit on it, and labels live in dark tooltips on hover. The active node always shows its tooltip. Pros: stays narrow, scales nicely from 3 → 5 sections without changing footprint. Cons: uses tooltip pattern which is a touch heavier than label-on-hover.
