Unreal Engine Personal

First project after switching to Unreal Engine — mostly an exploration of how the engine fits together. I was impressed but overwhelmed at first by the sheer surface area of tools and features.

What I worked on:

  • Basic action combat — nothing fancy, but it works end to end.
  • The full pipeline, all the way through to a packaged, playable demo.

What I learnt:

  • Unreal's gameplay framework, project workflow, and editor UI/UX.

That last one is genuinely the hardest part of picking up Unreal — there's so much in front of you on day one (frameworks, editors, build pipelines, Blueprint vs C++) that I spent the first few weeks just figuring out where everything lived.
