Third-year university coursework, designed for both PC and mobile. I kept the scope deliberately small after Fake Knight — single-genre, finite content, finishable in a semester.

What I worked on:

  • Top-down shooting combat.
  • Level flow and character management.
  • Enemy AI across a few different archetypes.
  • Save/load between sessions.
  • Three game modes — defeat all enemies, reach a target location, or survive over a set duration.

What I learnt:

  • Mobile is its own kind of complexity — input, performance, screen sizes, packaging.

A lot of those constraints only show up once you're testing on-device or building the final package. If you haven't accounted for them from day one, you end up redoing whole systems.
